# Transformers & Switchgear (T&D)

## 1. Definition & boundary (NAICS)

- **NAICS 335311** (Power, Distribution, and Specialty
  Transformer Manufacturing) and **NAICS 335313** (Switchgear
  and Switchboard Apparatus Manufacturing).
- Covers **large power transformers (LPT)** for transmission
  (>100 MVA), **distribution transformers** (DT) for last-mile,
  **pad-mount and pole-mount transformers** for utilities, and
  **gas-insulated switchgear (GIS)** for substation switching.
- Excludes power generation equipment (covered in
  `power-generation-oem.md`) and the utility itself
  (T&D utilities are in the value chain but not in this
  brief).

## 2. Demand drivers (with units)

- **Data center electrification** — hyperscaler load growth
  is the dominant near-term driver. LPT lead times have
  stretched from 12-18 months pre-pandemic to **128+ weeks
  in 2025** per multiple industry reports; some specialty
  quotes reach 200 weeks. A single 1 GW data center campus
  may need 5-10 LPTs.
- **Aging US grid** — average US transformer age 30-40
  years; ~70% of US LPTs are >25 years old per DOE.
- **Renewable interconnection** — wind + solar farms
  require step-up transformers; IRA-driven renewable
  build is incremental demand.
- **Reconductoring / grid hardening** — FERC Order 1920
  (transmission planning) and DOE Grid Resilience &
  Innovation Partnership (GRIP) program funding drive
  multi-year transformer demand.
- **EV charging** — fast-charging infrastructure requires
  distribution transformers; incremental demand.

## 3. Supply side: capacity, lead times, utilization

- **Hitachi Energy (private, Hitachi 8035.T + ABB
  divestiture stake)** — #1 LPT supplier globally; >70%
  global LPT market share historically; multi-year wait
  list.
- **GEV** (GE Vernova) — #2-#3 globally; power
  transformer and switchgear.
- **Siemens Energy (ENR.DE)** — #2-#3; Smart
  Infrastructure division.
- **Schneider Electric (SU.PA)** — distribution
  transformers + switchgear; not at LPT scale.
- **Eaton (ETN)** — distribution transformers,
  switchgear; strong US domestic presence.
- **Hubbell (HUBB)** — distribution transformers;
  US-listed.
- **Mitsubishi Electric (6503.JP)** — LPT, switchgear;
  Japan-listed.
- **Toshiba (6502.JP)** — LPT, switchgear; Japan-listed.
- **ABB (ABBN.SW)** — switchgear + drives + power
  products; LPT is via Hitachi JV.
- **Lead time** — LPT 128-200 weeks (2025); distribution
  transformer 50-80 weeks; small distribution <12 months
  (was historically).

## 4. Chokepoints

- **Grain-oriented electrical steel (GOES)** — the
  primary upstream chokepoint. Cleveland-Cliffs (CLF)
  is the only US GOES producer; globally Posco (005490.KS),
  Nippon Steel (5401.JP), Thyssenkrupp, and Baoshan
  (600019.SS) are major producers. US Section 232
  investigation launched 2025 on electrical steel imports.
- **Copper** — windings, busbars, cables. FCX, SCCO are
  the major mining suppliers; Aurubis (NA9.DE) and
  Southwire are major downstream processors.
- **Insulation** — 3M, DuPont, Weidmann (private) supply
  transformerboard / Nomex / Mylar.
- **LPT manufacturing capacity** — 4-5 major LPT OEMs
  globally; all are capacity-constrained.
- **Skilled labor** — transformer winding is a craft
  skill; an LPT can take 6-12 months to wind and assemble.
- **Tariff / trade** — Section 232 on electrical steel
  (2025), Section 301 on Chinese transformers, EU CBAM
  compliance all add 10-20% to US LPT landed cost.

## 6. Lead indicators (track daily/weekly)

- **EIA transformer data** — EIA-860 plus a new
  transformer-specific data collection (in development
  2025-2026).
- **Wood Mackenzie / EEI transformer lead time
  surveys** — quarterly.
- **HUBB / ETN / GEV / Schneider / Siemens Energy
  backlog** — quarterly, the cleanest read.
- **Section 232 / Section 301 tariff updates** —
  Federal Register.
- **DOE Transformer Procurement / Strategic
  Transformer Reserve** — DOE-issued RFPs and awards.
- **Cleveland-Cliffs GOES pricing** — quarterly earnings.
- **LME copper price** — daily; the cost side of
  transformer manufacturing.

## 7. Open questions / data gaps

- **The 200-week LPT lead time** — often cited in
  trade press; the official 128-week figure is more
  commonly cited. The 200-week figure may apply to
  specialty large units (765 kV class) only; needs
  primary-source confirmation.
- **US domestic LPT capacity** — currently ~50% of
  US LPT demand is imported. DOE issued
  transformer-specific funding in 2024-2025 to
  re-shore; new capacity is 18-36 months forward.
- **Tariff impacts** — Section 232 (electrical
  steel, 2025) and Section 301 (Chinese
  transformers, ongoing) — exact tariff rates and
  exemptions are still being finalized in 2026.
- **CBAM (EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)**
  — applicable to imported electrical steel and
  transformers from 2026; exact cost impact TBD.
- **HVDC transformer demand** — HVDC transmission
  (e.g., the SOO Green HVDC link, Champlain Hudson
  Power Express) requires specialty HVDC converter
  transformers; Hitachi Energy + GE have dominant
  positions; ramping demand.

## 8. Momentum & direction

Direction over the last 6 months: **PEAKING, with the
first sign of deceleration in the LPT order book
length**. This is the single most-cited near-term
bottleneck in the US AI buildout, and the relief
pipeline is becoming more visible.

Leading indicators pointing toward PEAKING:

- **LPT lead time** at 128+ weeks (some quotes
  200 weeks) has held through Q1 2026, but the rate
  of further lead-time expansion has slowed — i.e.,
  the curve is plateauing.
- **HUBB / ETN / GEV / Schneider / Siemens Energy
  backlog** all at record levels; Q1 2026 commentary
  showed the rate of new-order growth decelerating
  from the 2024-2025 surge.
- **Hitachi Energy** is the dominant LPT supplier
  with >70% global market share; multi-year wait
  list is structural.
- **Cleveland-Cliffs GOES** pricing has held; the
  Section 232 investigation (launched 2025 on
  electrical steel imports) is in progress.

Leading indicators pointing toward the relief
pipeline becoming more visible:

- **DOE Transformer Procurement / Strategic
  Transformer Reserve** funding 2024-2025; new
  US domestic LPT capacity 18-36 months forward.
- **Cleveland-Cliffs** new GOES line targeted for
  Q4 2026 commissioning (named expansion project
  in 2025 capex guidance).
- **Posco** GOES capacity expansion H1 2027.
- **Hitachi Energy** capacity expansion (US, EU,
  Brazil) commissioned through 2025-2027.
- **GEV** power transformer capacity expansion at
  multiple US sites.
- **Hubbell** distribution transformer capacity
  expansion.
- **Eaton** distribution transformer + switchgear
  capacity expansion.

`B'` reading (subjective, 6mo backward look): **roughly
+5 to +15** at the segment level. The LPT lead time
has held; the order book is at record; but the rate
of further deterioration has slowed. The segment is
plateauing at peak tightness.

## 9. Resolution timeline

The binding relief valves:

- **Hitachi Energy** LPT capacity expansion (US
  + EU + Brazil) — commissioned through 2025-2027;
  load-bearing 2027 supply event.
- **GEV** power transformer capacity expansion at
  multiple US sites — first waves H2 2026, full
  effect 2027.
- **Cleveland-Cliffs** new GOES line — Q4 2026
  commissioning; this is the upstream relief for
  the GOES chokepoint.
- **Posco** GOES capacity — H1 2027.
- **Hubbell** distribution transformer capacity
  expansion — through 2026-2027.
- **Eaton** distribution transformer + switchgear
  capacity — through 2026-2027.
- **DOE Strategic Transformer Reserve** — first
  deliveries 2026-2027; ~$250M in funding for US
  transformer production.

Demand-side relief:

- **Hyperscaler capex moderation** — none yet in
  2026. Watch 2027 guidance.
- **Sovereign AI capex** — adds demand, not relief.
- **Behind-the-meter generation** — bypasses the
  grid; not a transformer demand relief per se.
- **PJM / ERCOT / CAISO interconnection queue
  reform (FERC Order 2023)** — multi-year
  implementation; not a 2026-2027 relief.

ETA bucket: **>24 months** for full resolution. The
128+ week LPT lead time means even if a 2026
order is placed today, delivery is 2028-2029.
The Cleveland-Cliffs GOES line + Hitachi Energy
+ GEV capacity expansions bring the segment to
STABLE by H2 2027 / H1 2028.

## 10. Regime call

**Regime: PEAKED** (segment tightness at peak; the
rate of further deterioration has slowed; the
relief pipeline is becoming visible).

Confidence: **Medium-High**. This is a clean PEAKED
call. The 128+ week LPT lead time has held but
not extended further; the order book is at record
but the order intake rate is decelerating; the
relief pipeline (Cleveland-Cliffs GOES line,
Hitachi Energy, GEV, Eaton, Hubbell) is becoming
visible. The remaining uncertainty is in the
*timing* of the GOES line Q4 2026 commissioning
and the Hitachi Energy capacity ramp; both are
named 2026-2027 events with high conviction.

This segment is **NOT** in RESOLVING, so it remains
fully eligible for the long basket. For the short
basket, B × |B'| is high (high B with mildly
positive B').

Watch-items that would flip the regime: (a)
Cleveland-Cliffs GOES line Q4 2026 commissioning
delay, (b) Hitachi Energy capacity expansion
slip, (c) any major transformer OEM bankruptcy or
M&A event, (d) a 2027 hyperscaler capex cut, (e)
Section 232 tariff outcome (if it raises GOES
import cost, the relief pipeline extends).
