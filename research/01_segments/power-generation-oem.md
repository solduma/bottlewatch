# Power Generation OEM

## 1. Definition & boundary (NAICS)

- **NAICS 333611** (Turbine and Turbine Generator Set Units
  Manufacturing) for gas and wind turbines; **NAICS 2211**
  (Electric Power Generation, Transmission, and Distribution)
  for utility-owned generation; **NAICS 3345** (Navigational,
  Measuring, Electromedical, and Control Instruments
  Manufacturing) for grid controls.
- Covers **gas turbines** (GEV, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi
  HI), **nuclear steam supply and SMRs** (Westinghouse,
  BWXT, Holtec, NuScale, X-energy, TerraPower), **wind
  OEMs** (Vestas, Siemens Gamesa, GEV, Goldwind), **solar +
  inverters** (First Solar, Jinko, Trina, Enphase, Array
  Tech), and the **rare earths upstream** (MP, Lynas, USA
  Rare Earth).
- Excludes T&D utilities (covered in
  `transformers-tnd.md` / `td_utilities.md` analogues) and
  transformers themselves (covered in
  `transformers-tnd.md`).

## 2. Demand drivers (with units)

- **Hyperscaler AI data center MW demand** — IEA projects
  data center electricity demand at 2-3% of global
  electricity in 2025, doubling to 4-6% by 2030.
- **US data center load growth** — EPRI / NERC studies
  project 5-9% CAGR for US data center electricity demand
  through 2030.
- **GEV gas turbine backlog** — record order book through
  2026-2027 driven by data center buildout (e.g., GE
  Vernova's HA-class turbines).
- **SMR / AP1000 pipeline** — first commercial SMRs
  targeting late 2020s deployment; pipeline ~25-40 GWe
  globally (estimate, IAEA + vendor disclosures).
- **Renewable + storage** — hyperscaler PPAs increasingly
  bundled with battery storage (8-24h duration).

## 3. Supply side: capacity, lead times, utilization

- **GE Vernova (GEV)** — gas turbine + wind; spun out of
  GE in April 2024; AI-driven gas turbine orders are the
  dominant growth driver.
- **Siemens Energy (ENR.DE)** — gas turbines (SGT-9000HL)
  + grid technology; backlog at record levels.
- **Mitsubishi HI (7011.JP)** — gas turbines; M501JAC
  and the M501G series.
- **Westinghouse (private, Brookfield / Cameco
  ownership)** — AP1000 reactor; the only operational
  Gen III+ design; multiple units in construction
  globally (Vogtle 3/4, China, Eastern Europe).
- **BWXT (BWXT)** — naval nuclear + SMR (BWXT
  Advanced Reactors).
- **Holtec (private)** — SMR-160, SMR-300 designs;
  Holtec also has a major role in used fuel storage.
- **NuScale (SMR)** — VOYGR SMR design; Carbon Free
  Power Project (CFPP) cancelled November 2023, but
  international customers (Romania, Poland) advancing.
- **X-energy (private)** — Xe-100 high-temperature gas
  reactor; Dow Chemical as first customer.
- **TerraPower (private, Bill Gates)** — Natrium sodium-cooled
  fast reactor under construction in Kemmerer, WY;
  targeted 2030 operation.
- **Vestas (VWS.CO)** — wind OEM, #1 globally; offshore
  V236-15.0 MW turbine.
- **Siemens Gamesa (private, Siemens Energy owns)** —
  wind OEM; restructuring.
- **First Solar (FSLR)** — thin-film CdTe solar
  panels; preferred by US hyperscalers.
- **JinkoSolar (JKS)** — top-3 global module supplier
  (crystalline silicon).
- **Trina Solar (688599.SS)** — top-3 module supplier.
- **Enphase (ENPH)** — microinverters + battery
  storage.
- **Array Technologies (ARRY)** — solar trackers.
- **Lead time** — gas turbine lead times have stretched
  to 4-6 years for large utility-scale HA-class units
  (estimate, multi-vendor). Wind: 18-30 months.
  Solar: 6-12 months. SMRs: first-of-a-kind, 4-6 years
  for new builds.

## 4. Chokepoints

- **Gas turbine manufacturing capacity** — single biggest
  binding constraint on near-term AI power. GEV, Siemens
  Energy, MHI are all capacity-constrained.
- **SMR fuel supply** — HALEU (high-assay low-enriched
  uranium) supply is constrained; Centrus Energy
  (LEU) is scaling but capacity is gated.
- **Rare earths** — NdFeB magnets for wind turbine
  gearboxes and EV motors; MP Materials is the only
  scaled US producer; Lynas (Australia/Malaysia) is
  the other.
- **Solar panel capacity** — First Solar, Jinko, Trina
  capacity is being expanded but is constrained by
  US IRA-driven domestic-content rules and Section
  201/301 tariffs.
- **Permitting / regulatory** — FERC, NRC, BSEE, state
  PUCs all add multi-year permitting timelines. FERC
  Order 2023 (interconnection queue reform) is in early
  implementation.

## 6. Lead indicators (track daily/weekly)

- **GEV quarterly backlog and orders** — the cleanest
  read on gas turbine demand.
- **Siemens Energy backlog** — quarterly IR.
- **FERC interconnection queue length** — published
  monthly/quarterly; the upstream power constraint.
- **Hyperscaler PPA announcements** — public press
  releases (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta).
- **EIA-860 generator inventory** — published monthly;
  the canonical US generation capacity database.
- **EIA Short-Term Energy Outlook** — published
  monthly; data center load forecast updates.
- **SMR licensing milestones** — NRC design certifications
  and combined license applications.

## 7. Open questions / data gaps

- **Data center load by region** — disaggregated
  hyperscaler load by ISO/RTO is hard to source
  directly; EIA-860 does not yet break out
  hyperscaler-specific MW.
- **Gas turbine ASP trajectory** — sell-side channel
  checks suggest >$2,000/kW for HA-class, up from
  $1,000-1,200/kW pre-2022; needs primary
  verification.
- **SMR commercialization timeline** — TerraPower
  Natrium (2030), X-energy Xe-100 (late 2020s),
  Holtec SMR-300 (early 2030s), NuScale VOYGR
  (international, late 2020s); each has different
  licensing state. Conviction requires reading
  NRC filings directly.
- **Hyperscaler nuclear PPAs (Table Mountain, etc.)** —
  the Three Mile Island restart (Microsoft-Constellation)
  is the test case; structure of future PPAs (capacity
  payment vs. PPA price) is the load-bearing financial
  variable.

## 8. Momentum & direction

Direction over the last 6 months: **PEAKING at the gas
turbine layer, EMERGING at the SMR / AP1000 / nuclear
restarts layer, STABLE at the wind / solar layer**. The
segment is the power-track's most visible and is also
the segment with the most divergent sub-layer regimes.

Leading indicators pointing toward PEAKING at the gas
turbine layer:

- **GEV backlog** at record levels through 2026-2027;
  Q1 2026 commentary reaffirmed the 4-6 year HA-class
  lead time (estimated).
- **Siemens Energy backlog** similar pattern.
- **Mitsubishi HI** M501JAC + M501G orders up
  materially; lead times stretching.
- **Gas turbine ASP** reportedly >$2,000/kW for
  HA-class, up from $1,000-1,200/kW pre-2022; pricing
  has held, indicating continued buyer urgency.

Leading indicators pointing toward EMERGING at the
SMR / AP1000 / nuclear restart layer:

- **Three Mile Island restart** (Microsoft
  -Constellation) — 2028 targeted; first major
  US nuclear restart; structural relief for the
  2028+ outlook.
- **TerraPower Natrium** (Kemmerer, WY) — under
  construction; 2030 targeted operation.
- **Kairos Power** (TVA) — 2030+ targeted.
- **X-energy Xe-100** (Dow Chemical) — 2030+
  targeted.
- **Holtec SMR-300** — early 2030s targeted.
- **NuScale VOYGR** — international customers
  (Romania, Poland) advancing; Carbon Free Power
  Project (CFPP) cancelled November 2023.

Leading indicators pointing toward STABLE at the
wind / solar layer:

- **Vestas offshore V236-15.0 MW** turbine orders
  are growing but at a single-digit % rate.
- **First Solar thin-film** orders are growing
  with hyperscaler PPA demand.
- **Solar panel supply** is being expanded but
  is constrained by US IRA-driven domestic-content
  rules and Section 201/301 tariffs.

`B'` reading (subjective, 6mo backward look): **roughly
+10 to +20** at the segment level. The gas turbine
layer is still tightening; the SMR / nuclear restart
layer is an EMERGING demand-and-supply event; the
wind / solar layer is stable. The aggregate is
mildly tightening.

## 9. Resolution timeline

The binding relief valves:

- **GEV HA-class gas turbine ramp** — record backlog
  implies 2026-2027 commissioning of new units; the
  supply ramp is faster than SMR / nuclear but
  slower than the silicon track.
- **Siemens Energy SGT-9000HL** — same pattern.
- **Mitsubishi HI M501JAC / M501G** — same pattern.
- **Three Mile Island restart** — 2028; 837 MW
  clean firm capacity to Microsoft.
- **Vogtle 3 / 4 (already commissioned)** — adding
  ~2.2 GW to the US nuclear fleet; absorbed into
  Georgia Power + adjacent.
- **SMR / AP1000 commissioning** — 2030+; not a
  2026-2028 relief.
- **Behind-the-meter generation** (gas turbines at
  data center sites) — modular, 18-30 month lead
  time, complements the grid.

Demand-side relief:

- **Hyperscaler capex moderation** — none yet in
  2026. Watch 2027 guidance.
- **Sovereign AI capex** — adds demand, not relief.
- **Behind-the-meter economics** — hyperscalers
  increasingly self-supply via PPAs, which adds
  to the OEM backlog but does not reduce the
  absolute MW demand.

ETA bucket: **>24 months** for full resolution at
the gas turbine layer; **>24 months** for the SMR /
nuclear restart layer. The segment is structurally
PEAKING with no 2026-2027 relief.

## 10. Regime call

**Regime: PEAKING** (gas turbine layer still
tightening); **EMERGING** (SMR / nuclear restart /
behind-the-meter PPAs adding capacity but not yet
at meaningful scale); **STABLE** (wind / solar
supply / demand balance).

Confidence: **High** on the gas turbine PEAKING
call; **Medium** on the SMR / nuclear restart EMERGING
call (timing uncertainty in 2028-2030 window is large).

This segment is **NOT** in RESOLVING, so it remains
fully eligible for the long basket. For the short
basket, B × |B'| ranks the gas turbine layer among
the highest in the value chain.

Watch-items that would flip the regime: (a) Three
Mile Island restart acceleration, (b) gas turbine
ASP softening (would signal peak), (c) any major
SMR licensing milestone, (d) hyperscaler capex cut
in 2027 guidance, (e) Section 232 / IRA
modification that changes gas vs. nuclear /
renewables economics.
